30-second TL;DR

This is Kevin's complete Spotify Extended Streaming History — every play event from his first logged track in 2018 through May 2026 — parsed into a taste-and-behavior profile. Across the window he listened to 3,467 hours (144 full days) of music spanning 5,122 artists and 12,842 distinct tracks, on 2,386 different days. The all-time #1 artist is ILLENIUM (87 hours, 2,094 plays); the #1 track is Benson Boone's "Beautiful Things" (216 plays).

The core sound is melodic EDM / future-bass (ILLENIUM, Gryffin, Seven Lions, Martin Garrix, Kygo) fused with emotive pop and heartbreak balladry (Benson Boone, Teddy Swims, Lewis Capaldi, Ed Sheeran) — with eclectic detours into classic rock, UK rap, and reggaeton along the way.

Core insight: ILLENIUM is the spine of the taste, but the behavior is the real signature — Kevin is a completionist, anti-shuffle listener. Only 7% of plays are shuffled, 72% of tracks play to completion, and the skip rate is just 12%. He builds and replays curated lists; he doesn't let the algorithm drive.

The clever bits (and the honest snags)

The timezone trap

Every ts in the export is UTC. Bucketing by raw hour would smear the daily shape by 4–5 hours and put the "peak" in the wrong place. Re-zoning each event to America/New_York before bucketing is what makes the 6pm peak real instead of an artifact.

🐛 Where the draft and the data disagreed

The earlier draft pegged the biggest single-day binge at 105 Måneskin plays and counted 11,747 distinct tracks. Re-running the numbers from scratch put the Måneskin binge at 103 (a James Arthur day in 2021 actually edges it at 104), and counted 12,842 distinct tracks — the gap comes from how remix/feature variants collapse. Where the draft and a clean re-run disagreed, this report uses the re-run.

What's not here

Podcasts and audiobooks barely register (~10 hrs total) — that listening lives elsewhere. And 8,043 plays carry a not_applicable platform (offline syncs and cast hand-offs), folded into "Other / offline" rather than dropped, so the hours still add up.

Things the data quietly knew

2019 was a heartbreak record. Lewis Capaldi, Billie Eilish, FLETCHER, SHAED — and a single day (Jan 31, 2019) with 68 plays of Alice Fredenham. That's not a playlist, that's a mood.
The 2020 Bon Jovi detour is unmistakable. 402 plays in a year with zero EDM at the top — including a 79-play "Livin' on a Prayer" kind of day on Feb 29, 2020, and another 61-play run on Apr 3. A genuine classic-rock phase.
2023 was when the gym took over the queue. Steven Rodriguez (485 plays), Vin Jay, 2nd Life — hard, percussive, phonk-adjacent EDM, with an 86-play stretch on Dec 10. The kind of music you only loop while lifting.
The 2025 Benson Boone binge ran into December. 73 plays on Dec 18 and 67 on Dec 14, 2025 — two single days that each out-listen most people's entire week.
Travel left fingerprints. A few hundred plays in Great Britain, plus dozens scattered across Guatemala, Puerto Rico, Taiwan, and Japan — the connection country quietly logging where the trips went.
Anti-shuffle to the core. 93% of plays were in deliberate order, and 72% played to the very last second. This is a listener who curates, not one who lets the algorithm pick.
