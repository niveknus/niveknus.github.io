Why a separate "cheat-sheet" folder instead of just using NotebookLM?
NotebookLM keeps every class in its own sealed box — great for focus, but it can't look across all your classes at once, and the free version won't let you pull your stuff back out. So we keep our own copy of the important bits in a folder you control. That copy is what ChatGPT reads.

2 How everything connects

Your class materials already live in Google Drive. A small helper turns them into cheat-sheets, and ChatGPT reads those.

Materials you already keep in Drive → the helper makes cheat-sheets → ChatGPT reads them.

3 What the "helper" actually does

It's a small script. Plain-English version of its job, start to finish:

  • Looks inside each class folder in DriveReads the things that are already there: your typed notes, the class AI summary, the chat transcript (and later, the slide text).
  • Writes one tidy "cheat-sheet" per classA short file like XYZ123 — Digest.md with sections: Homework, Summary, My Notes, Highlights. Stamped with the course code and which track it's in (Required / Elective / Certification).
  • Saves them to a folder you ownA new "Curriculum Digests" folder in your Drive. It never changes the school's original files — it only reads them.
  • Bundles them by semesterChatGPT Projects only allow 25 files, and you'll have ~50 classes — so it groups each term's cheat-sheets into one file. ~4–8 files total. Easy.
How it gets run: Kevin runs it (one command) — at the start, then again whenever you've added new classes. It's not something you have to babysit. Later it can be set to refresh on its own.

4 What happens when you add a new class

This is the part that keeps it alive for two years. The cycle is the same every time:

📚 New Saturday class → 🗂️ New NotebookLM notebook
(for deep study) → ⚙️ Re-run the helper → 💬 ChatGPT now knows it too

Your per-class notebooks and your "ask everything" ChatGPT stay in sync — because both are fed from the same Drive materials.

5 How you'll use it day to day

For one class → open that class's NotebookLM notebook. (“What was my homework for XYZ123?”)

Across the whole curriculum → ask your ChatGPT Project:

“Which classes covered attachment theory?”
“Make me a study guide that connects my trauma classes.”
“What homework is still open across all my Required classes?”
“Explain how the certification material builds on what I learned in semester 1.”

If ChatGPT needs more detail than the cheat-sheet has, it'll point you to the right class — and you open that NotebookLM notebook for the deep version.

6 Why you're never trapped

💵 No new subscription

NotebookLM is free, your Drive is free, and ChatGPT is the one you already pay for. Nothing new to sign up for.

📁 You own the files

The cheat-sheets are plain text in your own Drive. They're yours during the PhD and long after.

🔄 Swap tools anytime

Today ChatGPT reads them. Tomorrow it could be Claude, Gemini, or anything else — same files, no rebuild.

🧩 Nothing locked inside an app

Your knowledge doesn't live trapped inside NotebookLM or ChatGPT. It lives in your folder. The apps just read it.
