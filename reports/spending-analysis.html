The bottom line first

Here's the honest read: you're not reckless. Your spending isn't a story of blowing money on junk — it's two very different things stacked on top of each other. Most of your big numbers are lumpy "capital" purchases — a mattress, a TV, a PC build, tools, a Garmin — bought once and used for years. Those mostly earn their keep. The part worth attacking is quieter: a steady trickle of small, forgettable purchases and a few recurring convenience habits that add up to real money without ever feeling like a decision.

The one reframe: stop thinking "Am I spending too much?" and start sorting every purchase into capex (durable, infrequent, you'd buy it again) vs. drift (small, impulsive, you won't remember it in a week). Your capex is mostly fine. Your drift is ~$2,000/yr on Amazon alone — and that's the money that can become savings without you feeling deprived.

The plan — how to spend less without feeling poorer

Add friction to the trickle
Turn off 1-Click. Use a 48-hour cart rule for anything non-essential under $50 — if you still want it in two days, buy it. Batch into one weekly order instead of daily taps. This alone targets the ~$2K/yr drift.
Move consumables off the trickle
Energy drinks, supplements, filters, household staples → buy in bulk (Costco / Subscribe & Save at the real discount), not as repeated full-price small orders. Cancel any Subscribe & Save you don't actively use.
Pre-budget your one big toy a year
You reliably buy ~1 large gear item annually (it's practically a tradition). Don't fight it — budget it. Pick a number (say $700), name what it's for, and let everything else be a no. Intentional capex isn't drift.
Hold the delivery win
You cut delivery ~$4K from 2024→2025. Keep it there. Your flavor profile shows your go-to orders — batch-cook the chicken/Mexican staples you already love and reserve delivery for genuine convenience, not default.
Automate the difference into savings
Don't "try to save what's left." The moment you cut a category, sweep that exact amount into your Wealthfront/savings on payday so it never sits in checking. Out of sight, out of spend.
Realistic 2026 target (G2 — save more): trim the sub-$25 drift (~$2,000) + bulk-buy the recurring consumables (~$500) + hold delivery at 2025 levels (vs. 2024 that's ~$4,000 already banked). Even ignoring the delivery win, ~$2,500/yr is redirectable to savings from the Amazon side alone — without touching anything you'd actually miss.
