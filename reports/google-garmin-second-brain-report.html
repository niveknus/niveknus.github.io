30-second TL;DR

The stuff you already generate is a goldmine — if you distill it

Every app quietly logs your life. This project pulled three of those logs — Google Takeout (Calendar + Maps), the on-device Maps Timeline, and the Garmin API — and turned raw rows into curated knowledge: a life timeline back to college, a master roster of the people in my orbit, a six-year fitness and weight arc, and a named map of where I actually go. Nothing was stored raw; everything was synthesized, cross-checked against what the wiki already knew, and linked together.

The core principle: synthesis-on-archive

Don't dump 2,712 rows into your notes. Distill them into the ~40 things that matter, write them in prose, cross-reference them, and verify against what you already know — flagging conflicts instead of overwriting them. The raw export stays on disk; the knowledge goes in the brain.

How it works + war stories

The clever bits (and the honest snags)

🗺️ The Maps Timeline mystery

Google Takeout only returned ~1 year of location data. Turns out Google moved Timeline onto the phone in 2024 — so the full history lives in an on-device export, not Takeout. I pulled that… and it still only reached back to my new phone's setup date. The deep history was gone (old phone wiped). Lesson logged; we leaned on reviews, photos, and the calendar for the older years.

💸 Naming 147 places for $0

Map visits ship as opaque IDs (ChIJ…) with no names. A short script ran each through the Google Places API — 147 lookups sat comfortably under the free tier, so the whole thing cost nothing and named every visit.

⏱️ Dodging Garmin's rate limiter

Sleep, HRV, and heart-rate are per-day API calls — pulling 6 years daily would've triggered rate-limiting. The fix: tier the pull. Range calls for weight + steps (cheap), monthly sampling for slow-moving VO2/RHR, and daily data only for a recent window.

⚖️ A discrepancy I refused to paper over

My notes said the 2025 cut hit 165 lb. The Garmin weigh-ins show a 2025 low of ~172 — and the true all-time low (162.7) was back in 2020. Rather than overwrite either, I flagged the conflict in the wiki for me to reconcile. Trust beats tidiness.

Replicate it

Do this with your own data

Export everything you can. Google Takeout (Calendar, Maps), the on-device Maps Timeline from your phone, and your fitness platform's data. Drop it all in one inbox folder.
Triage before you ingest. For each source, decide what's rich enough to use as-is vs. what needs enrichment. Skip the noise (settings files, automated-question answers).
Resolve the opaque bits. Run map place IDs through the Google Places API (free at this scale); pull fitness history via the platform's API, tiered to avoid rate limits.
Synthesize, don't store. Distill into dated prose entries and cross-reference them. Build a people roster as a single "who is this?" lookup.
Verify against what you know. Cross-check new facts against existing notes and flag conflicts instead of overwriting — your memory and the data won't always agree.
Automate the refresh. Schedule a quarterly re-pull so the record keeps compounding instead of going stale.
