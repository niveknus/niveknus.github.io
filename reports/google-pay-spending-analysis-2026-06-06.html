The clever bits (and the honest snags)

🐛 The phantom $976 phone

The Pixel 3 XL shows up twice on the same day (Oct 29, 2018) at $976.43 each — which is why it tops the chart at $1,952.86. That's almost certainly one purchase plus a re-bill/duplicate, not two phones bought at once. The decade has exactly 2 refunds and 1 cancellation on record; rather than guess which charge to drop, the totals were left exactly as Google reported them — $12,044.71 is the literal sum of every "Complete" line, duplicates and all. Honest beats tidy.

One Visa, one decade

Of 414 transactions, 399 ran on the same card (Visa •••• 0719). The remaining handful are one-off cards and a single Google Play balance redemption — a remarkably stable payment setup for a 10-year span.

Do this yourself

1. Go to takeout.google.com and export Google Pay → "Google transactions" as CSV.
2. Each row is Time, ID, Description, Product, Payment method, Status, Amount. Parse the dollar value out of the "USD 19.99" amount field with a regex.
3. The app/product name hides in trailing parentheses of Description — pull it out to roll up by app. Group Product for the channel view.
4. Bucket by year for the trend, by app for the top-spend table, and flag anything billing monthly for the subscription stack. Charts are plain CSS bars — no libraries.
