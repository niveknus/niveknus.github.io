30-second TL;DR

Kevin travels like a foodie with a passport: city exploration, night markets, sunset viewpoints, and the local dish done right. 2025 was the breakout year — a three-country Asia run (Tokyo → Seoul → Taiwan), a two-week Egypt & Rome trip built around a Cairo wedding, and a Western Caribbean cruise to close it out. Underneath the headline trips is a quieter pattern: an almost gravitational pull back to San Diego and to Taiwan, where his mom lives.

The one-sentence profile: a food-first city traveler with two home-away gravities — San Diego for the coast, Taiwan for family — who in 2025 went fully international.

The signature trips — tap to open

🐫 Egypt & Rome — the two-week epicNov 2025
Built around a friend's wedding at the Hilton Pyramids Golf in Cairo, then a free fortnight that became a two-country journey. Cairo: the Grand Egyptian Museum, Saladin Citadel, Cairo Tower at golden hour, Khan el-Khalili, koshari at Abou Tarek, and Om Ali for dessert. Then Cairo → Rome for sunsets at the Pincio, the hunt for the city's best cacio e pepe (Felice a Testaccio the gold standard), and the Colosseum. Honest footnote: a bout of traveler's diarrhea bridged Cairo to Rome and turned into the trip's most documented sub-saga — rehydration salts, Diosmectal from a Roman farmacia, and a hard-won return to pasta.
🏯 Asia, three countries in one MayMay 2025
One trip, carry-on only: a week in Tokyo (a guided EF tour — Kamata onsen, Togoshi Ginza, Shibuya Sky), three days in Seoul (Gyeongbokgung, Hongdae nights, Myeongdong skincare hauls), and six days in Taiwan south-to-north (Kaohsiung → Tainan → Alishan/Fenchihu → Sun Moon Lake → Taipei). Night markets the whole way down. Picked up a cold in Tokyo and worse allergies than home — the price of a new continent's pollen.
🚢 Western Caribbean cruiseDec 2025
Royal Caribbean's Grandeur of the Seas with Crissy — Costa Maya, Cozumel, Belize, and Roatan. Chichén Itzá as the bucket-list shore day; Paradise Beach in Cozumel ($3 in, snorkeling off the sand, a red reef starfish); Belize reached by tender because the harbor's too shallow to dock. Onboard: a seven-cookie bake-off and a starfish or two. Tip learned: meclizine over dimenhydrinate for daytime seas.
🌲 Pacific Northwest with friendsApr 2025
Vancouver (Stanley Park seawall, Granville Island, Gastown nights, aburi sushi at Miku) into Seattle (Pike Place, the Great Wheel, Lime scooters, Beecher's mac & cheese). Cool, rainy, carry-on and a waterproof jacket.
⛷️ The Colorado ski circuit2025–2026
Steamboat Springs and Breckenridge on the Ikon Pass. Notable mostly for the one that got away: a March 2026 Steamboat trip canceled by a cold turned into a masterclass in working a Spot Pass Protection insurance claim (telehealth note, redacted statements, zero-days-used screenshot). The mountain you skip can still teach you something.
🎆 Vegas with Crissy — dam & lanternsOct 2025
Hoover Dam by day, the Rise Lantern Festival in the desert by night — Calvin Harris on the decks and a sky full of released lanterns. Red Rock Canyon's 13-mile loop filled a delayed-flight afternoon.
🌴 Florida home-turf getaways2025
The weekend rotation: jet skiing at John's Pass and a snorkel in the 72°F cave at Devil's Den, sunsets at Pass-a-Grille in St. Pete, giraffe feeding at Brevard Zoo, and a boat day in Sarasota with Crissy's family.

Things the travel log quietly knew

San Diego is the home he doesn't live in — 8+ trips, a named favorite sunset spot, and a ramen map deep enough to look like a local's.
2025 was the international tipping point. Before it, the passport was mostly Taiwan; in one year he added Japan, Korea, Egypt, Italy, and the Caribbean.
He travels carry-on, even to three countries. The Asia trip, the PNW trip — all packed light, all backpack-and-roller.
The food is the itinerary. Night markets in Taipei, cacio e pepe in Rome, koshari in Cairo, birria in Tijuana — the meals are planned before the sights.
Even the canceled trips earn their keep — the skipped Steamboat weekend became a documented playbook for travel-insurance claims.
