30-second TL;DR

Kevin's "Home" structure has quietly grown into a 43-device smart home — lights, cameras, speakers, a thermostat, a vacuum, even a smart lock — spread across ten rooms and eleven manufacturers. The single most data-rich piece is a 326 MB Nest history file: two years of every heating cycle, fan kick, setpoint nudge, and home/away switch. Parse it and the house tells on itself — it lives at 68°F when occupied, drifts to 78°F when empty, and is overwhelmingly a cooling machine.

Core insight: over two years the A/C ran 9,142 hours and the heat just 575 — a 16-to-1 cooling-to-heating ratio. This is the thermal signature of Florida, written in raw telemetry.

Two summers, one winter, zero doubt: cooling rises every June and crests in late summer (Aug 2025 alone: 888 hours of A/C — nearly 29 hours a day across a 31-day month, because Florida runs two compressors). Heating is a rounding error that only wakes up for a few January/February cold snaps. Hottest single A/C day on record: July 4, 2024 — 22.6 hours.

The clever bits (and the honest snags)

🐛 326 MB on a 3.9 GB box — don't json.load()

A naive load of the history file risks ballooning to several GB of Python objects and OOM-ing the VM. The fix: a hand-rolled streaming reader that scans for the "events": array and feeds JSONDecoder().raw_decode() one object at a time off a sliding buffer — constant memory regardless of file size.

🐛 The phantom 179,366 "events"

The baseline note counted 179,366 — but that's "timestamp" string occurrences, and each event carries several nested ones (state-change times, service stamps). The real envelope count is 66,785. Of those, ~15.7k live in a separate device_history[] branch and are mostly Samsung SmartThings API-access logs, not telemetry — so the 51,083 in the main structure history are the events that actually describe the house.

🐛 "LEAF" hours aren't runtime

The daily energy summaries expose three duration samples: LEAF, HEATING, and COOLING. LEAF is Nest's efficiency-badge metric, not actual compressor time — using it would have over-counted. Summing only the explicit COOLING/HEATING samples gives the honest 9,142h / 575h split.

Things the data quietly knew

The house has one heating temperature: 66°F, set the exact same way all 61 times it ran. There is no winter nuance here — there's barely a winter.
July 4, 2024 the A/C ran 22.6 of 24 hours — the single hardest-working day on record. Independence Day in Orlando is a cooling event.
Lights are 42% of every device (18 of 43). This is, at heart, a lighting rig with a thermostat attached.
A black Hyundai Ioniq 6 is the most-named vehicle across the AI Home Briefs — the household EV, recognized by name by its own driveway camera.
Only 3 of 71 home/away switches were manual. The house decides for itself whether anyone's home 96% of the time.
Across two years, cooling beat heating 16 to 1 — the cleanest one-number proof of "this house is in Florida" you could ask for.
