30-second TL;DR

Every food photo in your library was filtered on-device (Apple Vision), identified by a vision model (Gemini), and located from its GPS — then joined with your DoorDash and Uber Eats data. The result is a full picture of what you like to eat, split into dine-out, home-cooked, and delivery.

Core insight: you photograph your aspirational meals (Japanese, sushi, ramen, dim sum, Korean BBQ) but order in for convenience (Chick-fil-A, Chipotle, BBQ). The camera and the delivery app describe two different appetites.

The clever bits (and the honest snags)

Takeout scatters a photo and its GPS sidecar across different zips — culling one at a time silently lost ~9,000 sidecars until the pipeline pooled them. Apple Vision exhausted its file-descriptor limit at ~2,400 images and started failing silently (fixed: raised the limit, surfaced errors). Gemini's flagship hit a ~1,000/day quota mid-run and a preview model emitted malformed JSON — both caught because every classification was verified, never assumed.
