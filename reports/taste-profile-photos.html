30-second TL;DR

Starting from a raw Google Takeout dump, the pipeline drops videos, keeps photos + their GPS, filters to food on your Mac (Apple Vision — nothing uploaded), sends only confirmed food to a vision model for dish names, then reads each photo's GPS to place it. No delivery data here — this is purely what your camera knows.

Core insight: your camera is a travel-food diary. Of 1,540 dine-out photos, 916 were taken while traveling — San Diego, Taiwan, Rome, Cairo, Tokyo, Seoul, Bogotá, the Caribbean — vs 624 around Orlando. You photograph food most when you're somewhere new.

The honest accuracy notes

Three layers, three confidence levels. Dish & cuisine come straight from the photo — high confidence. Home-vs-out and city/region come from GPS — high confidence. The exact restaurant is the weak link: we pick the restaurant physically nearest the photo's GPS, which works for isolated spots with clean GPS but fails when you're traveling (the lookup grabbed San Diego's Javier's, Taiwan's Gang Yuan, Bogotá's Hache de Hamburguesa — places near where the photo landed, not necessarily where you ate) or in dense plazas where many restaurants sit within meters and indoor GPS drifts. Fix in progress: cross-reference your Google Maps Timeline (the places you actually visited) instead of nearest-neighbor.

The build's war stories

Takeout scatters a photo and its GPS sidecar across different zips — processing one at a time silently lost ~9,000 sidecars until the pipeline pooled them all. Apple Vision quietly exhausted its file-descriptor limit at ~2,400 images and started failing silently (caught, fixed). Gemini's flagship hit a ~1,000/day quota mid-run, and a preview model emitted malformed JSON — both caught because every classification was verified, never assumed.
