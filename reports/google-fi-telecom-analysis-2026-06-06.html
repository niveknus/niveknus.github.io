30-second TL;DR

The Google Fi export holds three very different stories. The account record is a 10-year hardware diary: a leap-day signup in 2016 and a steady march of nine devices, lately one new Samsung Galaxy Ultra almost every year. The usage log only retains the last six months — but in that window one single contact accounts for three-quarters of all phone time. And the voicemail archive — every message saved since 2016, transcribed here with a local speech-to-text model — is overwhelmingly recruiters, insurance quotes, vacation clubs, and home contractors. The personal messages are rare enough to stand out.

Core insight: the calls log is a relationship X-ray (one person = 48 of 64 talk-hours), and the voicemail box is a decade-long record of life admin — job hunts, a house, and an unrelenting tide of robocalls.

Fun findings

The number was born on a leap day. Sign-up timestamp: 2016-02-29. The account technically has only had ~3 "real" birthdays.
One contact ≈ the whole phone. 671 interactions and 48.3 talk-hours from a single 941 number — more than 10× the next-closest person and ~75% of all voice minutes.
2025 was peak robocall. 123 voicemails saved in a single year — more than 2016, 2018, and 2020 combined — almost all business/spam.
A Lockheed Martin job lead is sitting in the voicemail box from January 2017 — a fossil of the early-career engineering job hunt.
Spring is upgrade season. Five of the last six phones were activated in Feb–Apr.
