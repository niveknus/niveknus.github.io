30-second TL;DR

Amazon's Kindle export is dozens of telemetry CSVs — power-state logs, Wi-Fi pings, screensaver taps. Buried inside the noise are four signal files: the library catalog (what Kevin owns), reading sessions (when and how long he reads on the device), Whispersync annotations (highlights + Audible position), and Amazon's own behavior rollup (completion %, KU membership). Cross-referenced, they paint a clear portrait.

Core insight: Kevin's library is a career autobiography. 2010–2015 is pure aerospace/engineering coursework — Aerodynamics, Calculus, Advanced Engineering Math, SAT prep. From 2018 on it pivots hard into leadership and mindset (Jocko Willink ×4, Tim Grover, Sinek, Lencioni). And the reading style is honest: he opens books, samples and highlights them, then finishes most of them by ear on Audible — Kindle is the highlighting layer, not the reading surface.

The snags (an honest data-limitations note)

This export is more redacted and lopsided than it looks. Four caveats kept the analysis honest:

LimitationWhat it means
Highlight text isn't exportedWe know how many passages he marked and in which books, but not what they said. The actual quotes are unrecoverable from this data.noted
Kindle hours undercount badlyOnly 6.5 hours are logged because Kevin is audiobook-first — 15 titles carry Audible "last heard" markers (Range, Blink, Freakonomics, No Rules Rules, Die With Zero…). Kindle is his sampling/highlighting layer; Audible does the heavy lifting. This is not "Kevin barely reads."framed
~7% completion is a Kindle artifactAmazon's rollup pegs average percent-read at ~7% (max ~13%) — because completion is measured on the device he opens books on, not the one he finishes them on. A sampler-not-finisher pattern, on Kindle specifically.framed
Library = owned, not readThe 75-title catalog mixes purchases, Kindle Unlimited borrows, duplicate formats, and one non-book (a Rocketbook reusable notebook). Ownership ≠ completion. Session times are also UTC, so "evening reader" is an inference.caveat

Things the data quietly knew

The textbook→self-help pivot isn't gradual — it's a cliff. The last engineering text lands in 2015; the leadership wave starts in 2018. The library is a snapshot of leaving school and entering a career.
Jocko Willink is the most-owned author with 4 titles — Extreme Ownership, Dichotomy of Leadership, Discipline Equals Freedom, and Leadership Strategy & Tactics. The whole Navy-SEAL leadership canon, collected.
Relentless is the most highlighted (13) and 2nd most-read by time (2.2h) — the book he engaged with hardest, pen in hand.
The independent Instagram-saves analysis flagged Sahil Bloom's The 5 Types of Wealth as a saved idea — and here it is, actually owned. Two separate data exports corroborating the same taste.
Reading sessions cluster in the evening (6pm–midnight UTC) — short, infrequent bursts, never a marathon. He's a Kindle Unlimited member who dips in, not a couch-bound binge-reader.
