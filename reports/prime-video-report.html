30-second TL;DR

This is Kevin's full Amazon Prime Video export, cleaned to his own viewing — 1,093 playback events, of which 579 are real Kevin content (the rest are ~25% auto-playing promos and trailers, plus all shared-account plays from old roommate/family logins in Tallahassee and Columbus, now stripped out). Two volume eras bracket a five-year silence: a 2015–16 24 marathon on a Windows 8.1 PC — much of it streamed from London during travel — then steady streaming on a Roku-connected LG OLED from 2023 onward. The through-line is unmistakable: when Kevin pays, he pays for blockbusters — 18 of 19 transactions are UHD rentals of Fast & Furious, Dune, and MCU tentpoles.

Core insight: Netflix is Kevin's serialized theater — charming criminals, spies, slow-burn heists. Prime Video is his 4K rental cinema — big-budget action he pays to watch in the best quality available. Same viewer, two completely different jobs-to-be-done.

The clever bits (and the honest snags)

🐛 War story: the regex that ate the show name

First pass anchored on the first hyphen and required " - Season N". That worked for modern Amazon Originals but quietly mangled the 2015 catalog: 24's episode titles are clock times ("6:00 A.M. - 7:00 A.M."), so a naive split grabbed "12:00 A.M." as the show name and reported 24 as having zero plays while inventing fake "shows" like "12:00 A.M.-24". The fix: strip the trailing (?: -)? Season N first, then take the show from the rightmost hyphen. (A later wrinkle: The Boys Season 4 uses an en-dash separator while Season 5 uses a hyphen, so the split had to accept both.) That recovered the real number — 216 of Kevin's own 24 plays — and collapsed the phantom shows down to a true count of 20.

🧹 Filtering the noise (twice)

A quarter of all rows (276 of 1,093) are auto-played promos and trailers, not things Kevin chose to watch. On top of that, a chunk of the early library was watched on shared logins in Tallahassee and Columbus — old roommate/family accounts, not Kevin — surfaced cleanly by the per-play City field. Both layers are stripped: every content metric here is Kevin's own viewing only.

🕒 Timezone honesty

All timestamps are UTC; the hour-of-day chart converts to US Eastern. Because Kevin streamed from London during travel and the export doesn't localize per-play, the hour shape is accurate to ±1 hour — fine for "midday vs. evening," not for the exact minute.
